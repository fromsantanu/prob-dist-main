## â­ **1. Expected Value (Mean) of Random Variables**

âœ… **Definition (Simple Words):**
The **expected value** is the **average value** a random variable takes **in the long run**.

ðŸ”¢ **Formula (Discrete Random Variable):**

$$

$$

* $x_i$ = possible values
* $P(x_i)$ = probability of each value

---

### ðŸŽ¯ **Example**

**Rolling a dice**

* Values: 1,2,3,4,5,6
* Each with probability = 1/6

$$
E[X] = (1+2+3+4+5+6) \times \frac{1}{6}
$$

$$
E[X] = \frac{21}{6} = 3.5
$$

âœ… **Meaning:** Average outcome **over many rolls is 3.5**.

---

### ðŸ’» **Python Example**

```python

```

âœ… **Standard Deviation (Ïƒ):**

### ðŸŽ¯ **Example**

**Rolling a dice**

* $E[X] = 3.5$

Calculate variance:

$$

## â­ **3. Higher Moments: Skewness and Kurtosis**

### ðŸ”¢ **(i) Skewness**

âœ… **Definition:** Measures **asymmetry** of distribution.

* **Positive skew:** Tail on right (e.g. income data)
* **Negative skew:** Tail on left

ðŸ”¢ **Interpretation:**

* Skewness = 0 â†’ **Symmetrical** (normal distribution)
* Skewness > 0 â†’ Tail on right
* Skewness < 0 â†’ Tail on left

---

### ðŸ”¢ **(ii) Kurtosis**

âœ… **Definition:** Measures **tailedness or peakedness** of distribution.

* **High kurtosis:** Heavy tails, sharp peak
* **Low kurtosis:** Light tails, flatter peak

ðŸ”¢ **Interpretation:**

* Kurtosis > 3 â†’ **Leptokurtic (sharper peak)**
* Kurtosis = 3 â†’ **Mesokurtic (normal distribution)**
* Kurtosis < 3 â†’ **Platykurtic (flatter peak)**

---

### ðŸ’» **Python Example (Skewness and Kurtosis)**

```python

# Calculate skewness
skewness = stats.skew(data)
print("Skewness:", skewness)

# Calculate kurtosis
kurtosis = stats.kurtosis(data, fisher=False)
print("Kurtosis:", kurtosis)
```

âœ… **Note:** `fisher=False` gives Pearsonâ€™s definition (kurtosis of normal = 3).

ðŸ”¹ **Formula:**

$$

* **First derivative at t=0 = Mean**
